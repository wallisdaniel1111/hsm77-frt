THE LOGIC BEHIND THE PLAN

BACKGROUND: SABERHAWKS77 is built upon the professional framework of Daniel Wallis, an ALL-NAVY team member and former NIKE Professional athlete. This program scales elite-level track and field preparation into a high-performance protocol specifically for the Navy PRT.

The Allostatic Advantage: Utilizing the McEwen Model of Allostatic Load to audit readiness. The program pivots in real-time based on sleep and stress inputs, ensuring high intensities are only prescribed when the physiology is capable of adaptation.

Neural Pace Priming: Success in the 1.5-mile run is determined by Lactate Threshold and Running Economy. By focusing on 800m repeats with specific 400m lap targets (e.g., 82s/lap), we prime your nervous system to recognize and maintain goal-pace rhythm without the metabolic collapse associated with "blind" running.

Lactate Clearance Logistics: Training at exactly 102% of goal pace forces the body to improve its ability to clear blood lactate while under movement stress. This ensures that the final 400m of your PRT is a controlled acceleration.

6-WEEK TACTICAL ROADMAP

Week 1: Adaptation

Neural Priming. Establishing the foundation for goal-pace rhythm and teaching the body the mechanics of interval recovery.

Week 2: Loading

Structural Stress. Incremental increase in interval volume to build mitochondrial density and aerobic strength.

Week 3: Development

Threshold Expansion. Pushing the lactate threshold further out through controlled overreaching and specific pace targets.

Week 4: Overreaching

Peak Load. The maximum growth stimulus phase, testing the intersection of maximum volume and allostatic pressure.

Week 5: Realization

Intensity Maintenance. Reducing junk volume while holding high-end speed to keep the nervous system sharp.

Week 6: Absorption

The Taper. Deliberate fatigue shedding to reveal the Day 42 peak performance physiological state.
